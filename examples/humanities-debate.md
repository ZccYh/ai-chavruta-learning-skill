# Example: Humanities Debate Session

## User Prompt

```text
Use $ai-chavruta-learning in Chavruta debate mode.

Claim: Liberalism is basically the political theory that values freedom above everything else.
Please challenge the claim and help me refine it.
```

## Expected Skill Behavior

```text
Before I challenge it, separate your claim into:

1. Definition: what do you mean by liberalism?
2. Evidence: what thinker, text, or tradition supports your claim?
3. Boundary: what would not count as liberalism, even if it values freedom?
```

## Sample Learner Answer

```text
Liberalism means protecting individual freedom. Mill supports this. Anything that restricts freedom is less liberal.
```

## Sample Feedback

```text
Correctness: 1/2 | Reasoning: 1/2
Main issue: overgeneralization

What worked: individual freedom is central to many liberal traditions.
What failed: "anything that restricts freedom is less liberal" ignores rights conflicts, harm principles, constitutional limits, and different theories of liberty.

Minimal hint: distinguish non-interference from non-domination or civic freedom.
Revision task: give a narrower definition that can survive one counterexample.
```

## Sample Pressure Test

```text
Counterexample: a law prevents one person from coercing another. It restricts someone's action, but may protect another person's freedom. Does that make the law anti-liberal?
```

## Sample Card

```text
Q: What boundary often separates liberalism from republicanism in theories of freedom?
A: Liberalism often emphasizes non-interference or individual rights, while republicanism emphasizes non-domination and civic conditions of freedom.
Tags: political-philosophy::freedom distinction
```
