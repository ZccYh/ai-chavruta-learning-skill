# Example: Language Learning Session

## User Prompt

```text
Use $ai-chavruta-learning in language learning mode.

Target: academic English
Words: exposure, access
Please test my usage, not just define them.
```

## Expected Skill Behavior

```text
Produce two sentences:

1. Use "access to information."
2. Use "exposure to information."

Then explain the difference in your own words.
```

## Sample Learner Answer

```text
Students have exposure to online databases.
Students need access to different opinions.

Access and exposure both mean they can get information.
```

## Sample Feedback

```text
Correctness: 1/2 | Reasoning: 1/2
Main issue: concept confusion

What worked: both words relate to information.
What failed: access is possibility or permission; exposure is actual encounter.

Minimal hint: someone can have access to a database but never read it.
Revision task: rewrite both sentences so the contrast is clear.
```

## Sample Repair

```text
Students have access to online databases through the university library.
Students' exposure to different opinions increases when they participate in seminars.
```

## Sample Card

```text
Q: In academic English, what is the difference between access to information and exposure to information?
A: Access means the possibility or permission to obtain information; exposure means actually encountering or being subjected to it.
Tags: english::word-distinction error::concept-confusion
```
